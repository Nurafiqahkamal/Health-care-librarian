Medical Librarian Job Description

Medical librarians perform a key task for both healthcare professionals and patients by managing the flow of medical information. They provide high-quality health data to patients and keep doctors and other medical experts up to date on the latest advances in their specialty, ensuring effective care and superior diagnoses.

  • Assist in training new healthcare providers in academic settings.
  • Utilize print and digital data tools to support medical professionals.
  • Support research teams in universities or pharmaceutical companies.
  • Conduct outreach programs via websites, blogs, and online education.
  • Retrieve biomedical data for daily clinical or research needs.
  • Act as informationists to teach and support data usage in healthcare.
  • Maintain Databases: Keep health data updated and organized.
  • Research: Stay informed on medical practices through study.
  • Critical Thinking: Adapt to changes and user needs.
  • Analytical: Evaluate complex information logically.
  • Problem Solving: Determine effective solutions for medical info needs.
  • Reading Comprehension: Understand and share health content accurately.
  • Communication: Work closely with healthcare teams to update them on advancements.

Medical librarians work in hospitals, universities, pharma companies, research centers, publishers, and government agencies. Their role is vital wherever medical data is needed.

They may serve in high-level academic or information roles such as deans or chief information officers, and many manage digital libraries or health databases for major hospitals and online platforms.
